Research Methodology for Rebranding Project: A Focus on User Feedback

As part of the rebranding project for “Geldrops MuziekCorps,” I initiated surveys to gather essential insights from our target audience. Recognizing the pivotal role of user feedback in shaping our rebranding efforts, I strategically designed two distinct surveys to delve into different aspects of our audience's preferences and concerns.

Survey 1: Understanding Barriers and Concerns

The first survey was meticulously crafted to uncover the factors hindering potential members from joining the orchestra and to pinpoint their specific apprehensions. Targeting individuals within our desired demographic, the survey comprised in-depth questions aimed at eliciting detailed responses. However, upon analysis, it became evident that the response rate was disappointingly low. Curious to understand this, I reached out to non-respondents and discovered a common thread: the length and complexity of the questions deterred them from participating. Many cited time constraints and a reluctance to engage with lengthy inquiries.

Survey 2: Streamlined Approach for Enhanced Engagement

In response to the feedback from Survey 1, I undertook a strategic shift in survey design for the second iteration. This time, the focus was on brevity and visual appeal. I carefully curated five concise, multiple-choice questions that incorporated visual elements such as color palettes and logo concepts. The aim was twofold: to make the survey more accessible and engaging while also gathering valuable feedback on design preferences.

Importance of Changes in Methodology

The decision to transition from long-form, open-ended questions to succinct, visually-oriented ones was pivotal. By reducing the cognitive load on respondents and offering them a quick, visually stimulating survey experience, we witnessed a remarkable increase in engagement. The 16 responses received for Survey 2, a significant improvement from the initial handful, underscored the effectiveness of this methodological shift.

Selection of Question Types

The choice of multiple-choice questions with visual elements was deliberate. Visual aids, such as color palettes and logo mock-ups, are potent tools for evoking immediate reactions and preferences. This approach not only facilitated a smoother survey experience for participants but also yielded clear, actionable data for our design team.

Iterative Process for Refined Design Choices

The iterative nature of these surveys is at the core of our design process. Each response, whether obtained through Survey 1 or Survey 2, serves as a building block for refining our design choices. By incorporating feedback directly from our target audience, we are ensuring that the final website design resonates with their preferences, effectively communicates our brand message, and enhances the overall user experience.

In conclusion, the methodological evolution from a comprehensive, text-heavy survey to a concise, visually-driven one exemplifies our commitment to user-centric design. By actively listening to our audience through these surveys, we are not only gathering valuable insights but also fostering a sense of inclusivity and collaboration in our rebranding journey. This meticulous attention to user feedback ensures that our final product will not only meet but exceed the expectations of our audience, establishing a strong and enduring brand identity for “Geldrops MuziekCorps.”
