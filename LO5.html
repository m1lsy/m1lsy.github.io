Improving Workflow

This semester, I put a large part of my focus on improving my workflow. Typically, I am a very disorganized person and I often struggle to meet deadlines. I have tried various techniques in the past to improve my organization and time management such as using productivity applications like Notion or even tasks as simple as making a “To-Do List”, but I have realized that these things do not work for me and often just end up being a waste of time.

I also prefer to just ‘dive into’ my work straight away and often find the process of planning and prototyping to be annoying. For example, I tend to start working on the final product and designing as I go rather than created a definite plan first. Although this is how I like to work, I have encounted many issues through working this way and so I knew I needed to change it.

What I did:

To stay on top of things, I decided to start working on my portfolio in the very beginning of the semester to ensure that I was not left with a lot of last minute work. I also ensured to keep my attendance high and to make use of OIL time aswell as attend lectures. I also starting creating wireframes and prototyping before beginning my final projects.

What i learned:

Through trying to change my work flow, I learnt the importance of consistency and that in order to change these habits I had to put in work every day. I also learnt that you should not “make things up as you go” as this often results in more work in the end.

How I think it went:

I deficiently noticed an improvement in my workflow this semester compared to last semester as I felt more organized and focused. I am no where near where I want to be but I believe that if I practice these good habits going forward I can drastically continue to improve in the future
