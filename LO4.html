PROJECT ORGINIZATION

Whilst working in groups, it is very important to form a solid foundation of rules and expectations for each other from the very beginning of the project. This is important as while working with people, it is common to run into problems as everyone has their own work ethics and their own time schedules. Some group members may have jobs and be unable to meet whilst others may have to leave the country for a few days, and so being organized is a very important factor.

WHAT I DID:

To ensure that my group did not run into any issues, I created a team charter to ensure we all had similar expectations and clarity on what we each wanted for this project. Together with my teammates, we also formulated a project plan which we would use to assign tasks to each other and let each other know how close we were to completing the tasks.

1st Project - Rebranding (Conducting Research)

Goals

For my rebranding project for “Geldrops MuziekCorps,” I aimed to gather insights from the target audience to understand factors preventing them from joining the orchestra and their concerns. Additionally, I wanted to gather feedback on design elements such as color palettes and logo ideas to ensure the final design resonated with our audience.

Methods

To achieve these goals, I used the Field Research Method and I created two surveys to gain a deeper understanding of my target audiences preferences and habits:

FIRST SURVEY:

Research Question: "What are the key concerns and barriers preventing individuals from joining the "Geldrops MuziekCorps" orchestra?"

This survey was aimed at the target audience of our project. It included detailed questions about their concerns and factors preventing them from joining the orchestra. Despite sending it to multiple people in the required demographic, I received only a handful of responses. When I enquired why people did not answer the survey, they mostly stated that they did not have time or did not want to answer in-depth questions. This feedback indicated that the questions were too long and required more time to answer than people were willing to spend.

SECOND SURVEY:

Research Question: "How do design preferences, such as color palettes and logo ideas, influence the perception and appeal of the "Geldrops MuziekCorps" rebranding among the target audience?"

Learning from the first survey, I designed a shorter survey with five multiple-choice questions that included less text and more visual aspects. This survey focused on design elements such as possible color palettes and logo ideas.

Results

The results from the first survey revealed that the factors preventing new members of the orchestra were cost of the membership and reputation of the orchestra. This told us to focus on advertising these two aspects when coming up with the design of the new GMC website. The second survey gave us clarity on what direction to go with our logo, with most people voting for a text based logo instead of an illustration. These results gave us great insights into the thought process of our target audiences preferences and thus was a great help in steering us in the right direction.

2nd Project - Media Campaign (Interviews)

Goals

For our Media Campaign, my team and I made use of an amazing opportunity to conduct interviews (using the Field Method) with High School students who are interested in applying to ICT at Fontys. This was an amazing opportunity as they perfectly fit the demographic of our target audience. Our goals for the interviews was to receive some honest feedback on their experience with the ICT website and what they felt was lacking about it, in order to create a final product that addressed their concerns and satisfies their needs.

Methods

Before thinking of questions to ask, we decided to formulate a research question:

"How do potential students perceive the usability and content of the ICT website, and what improvements do they suggest to enhance their overall experience?"

Formulating this research question first is important as it aids in focus and direction throughout the methodology.

Once we had our research question, we began to formulate the questions for the interviews, ensuring to only make a few, short, easy to interpret questions to avoid the High School Students losing interest in the interview or declining to take part all together. My teammates and I then went around the room and interviewed each student, making sure to jot down their answers and ideas.

What I did

To begin the creation of my Interactive To-Do List application, I started with research to ensure I was designing an application that satisfied the needs and desires of my target audience. I began my research process with context mapping, utilizing the FIELD method. This involved conducting research on existing UX articles as well as creating and administering interviews and surveys. After collecting all of this information, I compiled my findings into a comprehensive research report.

What I learned

From this research, I learned that understanding user needs and preferences is crucial for designing an effective interactive application. The insights gained from UX articles, interviews, and surveys highlighted key features and functionalities that users find valuable. Additionally, I discovered common pain points and areas for improvement in existing to-do list applications, which informed the design decisions for my project.
