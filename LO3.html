1st Project – Rebranding (Logo Design)

Project Overview

Logo design is something I find very challenging but really enjoy. For the rebranding project for “Geldrops MuziekCorps,” we were required to design a new logo that was modern but also resonated with the client. As shown in the illustrations below, the designing process was somewhat chaotic, with us playing around with any idea that came to mind.

INITIAL LOGO IDEAS FOR GELDROPSMUZIEK CORPS PROJECT ↗

Initial Challenges and Learning

Before working on this project, I had no experience using Illustrator, so it was quite challenging initially. I started by tracing some images of instruments to help familiarize myself with the program. I also attended an Illustrator Introduction workshop, which helped me navigate the program and understand the basics.

Iteration and Design Process

I learned the importance of iterating and always duplicating the design before adding to it. This practice allows you to see how the design has evolved and to have something to refer back to in case of errors. Throughout my design process, iteration is visible, with each design differing slightly from the previous one.

Feedback and Refinement

Throughout the design process, I received helpful feedback from the client, my teachers, and my peers. This feedback allowed me to view my work from a different perspective and helped me identify what was working and what was not. For example, I asked some of my teachers who specialize in design for feedback on some of the designs.

The feedback I received was positive as many liked my very stylized approach to the design.

Although the design worked visually, it was brought to my attention that logically, the direction of the trumpet did not make sense. If a person were to play the trumpet in my drawing, the sound would travel in the wrong direction.

With this feedback, I decided to think of ways that would make sense logically and visually.

Final Adjustments

I then created trumpet-inspired logo designs that resembled the instrument more realistically while still having the same stylized elements as my previous designs.

I then decided to get feedback from the client to see if this design resonated with them. The feedback I received was positive and that they found the first design to resonate with them as a company the most.

The only change they requested was for the trumpet to be a brighter yellow colour instead of the beige colour it was currently.

SPRINT X (INTERACTIVE TO-DO LIST DESIGN)

After conducting in-depth research into User Experience Design and User Preferences for To-Do List applications (read more in Learning Outcome 4), I began my design process by drawing up some basic wireframes for possible layouts. A key takeaway from my research was that users have different preferences for layouts, with some preferring tasks organized as a list, some preferring a Calander layout and others preferring the “post-it” note, analogue style. With this aspect in mind, I decided to create the option for the user to rearrange their tasks in different layouts, in order to find one which would best suit their preferences.

In my wireframe, I came up with a few ideas for how I could arrange these different layouts, inspired by other applications such as Miro and Asana.

Once I had drawn my wireframes, I showed them to my fellow students in order to get their input on the layout. The feedback they gave me was positive, and that they liked the option to change the layout to their preference.

The next step I took was creating some low fidelity prototypes in Figma, all super simple with no colour to get a general idea of the layout. After creating these, I conducted some user testing which you can read more about in Learning Outcome 1.

The feedback that I got from those who participated in the user tests was positive. The design flaws that were brought up were that the use ‘star icon’ (meant to be a way to favourite or prioritize a task) on the list layout was not clear. They suggested that I either make it another icon such as a ‘pin’ icon or that I make it bigger or a bright colour. They also felt that the tasks should be listed in date order (soonest – furthest).

Next, I created a High Fedelity Prototype in Figma and formulated a colour pallette to compliment my design and layout.

The inspiration behind my deisgn

My design work is always heavily influenced by the media I am consuming and I often take inspiration from other artists who I interact with. During the creation of this portfolio and over the semester, I was mainly listening to one of my favorite electronic artists, Aphex Twin, as I find this music to help me with concentration and increase my productivity. I looked at various album art and posters as well the information spreads which you receive when purchasing a CD or Vinyl for inspiration. The ‘Aphex Twin style’ has a very particular, simplistic look which I find to be really visually appealing but unique at the same time.

The album ‘Syro’ really caught my attention in particular due to its bright, unique colour. This colour is one that I have been seeing more of in the design world recently with similar ‘lemon-lime’ hues being featured on lists of colour trends in Graphic Deisgn for 2024. Another aspect which I discovered was that the HEX code for this colour is actually #BADA55 which I thought was a funny and clever easter egg.

Although I really loved this bright, unique colour as the main background of my website, I received some mixed feedback. Some of my peers and teachers appreciated it for its uniqueness and eye-catching quality. However, others pointed out that such a bright colour might not be ideal since pictures placed on top of it could clash. Despite this valid concern, I was still determined to keep the bright colour. To address potential issues with colour clashing, I explored ways to adjust my design and structure the layout more effectively.

In the images above, you can that I first tried to simply add a white background to certain sections to help reduce the colour clashing and to make the images 'pop-out' more. However, I felt that this was too harsh of a contrast and so I decided to rather lower the opacity and add some padding aswell as a border radius to soften it up. As you can see by the examples above, the colour clashing between the image and the background is significantly reduced. For images with more neutral colours, I left them directly on the background without any additional changes.

Logo Creation

In regards to my logo designs, I knew I wanted to make something eye catching that would cover my home page but also would work as a “home button” on all of my pages. I started by design process by drawing some ideas on paper and then I took to illustrator to play around with different effects and fonts.

I then decided to choose a chrome inspired style for my logos as it is a design trend I have been seeing a lot of recently and so I thought I would try it for myself. I first tried to create it on illustrator with the inflate effect but as seen in the image below, it did not work out.

I then chatted to my father and brother who both have some basic experience in Photoshop, and they suggested to me that I switch to creating my logo in Photoshop if I want to create the metal effect. So, I took their advise and began to play around with different effects until I found one which I liked. Once I had chosen my effect, I tried it out with different fonts as well as different versions of my name to see what looked best. I then decided on going with KELSI 1 FILL which is a bubble, graffiti style font which I think matched the style of the portfolio design well.

I then decided on creating two logos. One that just said “MILA” for the main element of my index page, and the other one a circle with “M” inside, heavily inspired by the Aphex Twin logo, to be used as a “Home Button”.
