Investigative Problem Solving

The learning outcome "Investigative Problem Solving" emphasizes the ability to approach problem-solving with a persistent sense of curiosity and a commitment to asking relevant questions at various stages of the solution process.

A pragmatic approach involves seeking practical and workable solutions, while critical thinking involves analysing information thoroughly and considering multiple perspectives. Additionally, relying on credible sources to inform your problem-solving process is crucial.
To successfully achieve this learning outcome and to practice the various approaches to problem solving, I made use of many sources to help me find the answers to questions that came up along the way, while creating my projects.

In order to gain an understanding as to what a “pragmatic approach to problem solving” is, I did some research and came across an article called “The Pragmatic Programmer” written by software engineer, Brandon Opere. In this article, Opere touches on the principles that are required in pragmatic programming which I found to be highly insightful. These principles include the DRY principle (meaning “Don’t Repeat Yourself”), and the KISS principle (meaning “Keep It Simple, Stupid”).
After reading these principles, I discovered that I often repeated myself in my code which is a bad practice and something that needs to be avoided. I thus went back and cleaned up my code in places where repetition was evident.

The reminder to “Keep It Simple” was also helpful as I feel that sometimes I can get carried away in over complicating what I am doing by trying to find new ways to do it, when in reality keeping it simple is much easier and more reliable.
Other examples where I practiced Investigative problem solving include using YouTube videos and websites for further in-depth explanation on topics that I could not grasp.

Some of the YouTube channels which aided me in finding solutions for my problems include “Web Dev Simplified”, “Kevin Powell” and “Net Ninja”. The websites which helped me include “We3Schools” and “CodeAcademy”. Having a variety of tutorials and explanation is a great advantage as this provides an array of different solutions to try.

Reflection:

Reflecting back on this learning outcome, I believe that Investigate Problem Solving is a very important aspect of the Professional Skills we learn as it allows for variety in the solutions we require for the problems we encounter. This has taught me that most of the time, there is more than one solution and that I should always explore the other options before accepting one solution to a problem and that there could be better or easier ways out there. This is a skill that will be carried with me into my next semesters.

Targeted Interaction

I believe that Targeted Interaction is about demonstrating my ability to engage directly with stakeholders during the process of producing projects and assignments. Some ways in which I have demonstrated this learning outcome are through aspects such as clear communication, taking an audience-centric approach to project work and active participation in group work. Through clear communication with both stakeholders and team mates, goals and expectations are made clear. This ensures that all members of the team understand their roles and responsibilities and avoids miscommunication.
With clear communication in place, we can tailor our work to suit the needs and expectations of the stakeholders, resulting in an audience-centric approach to working. Active participation in group work also plays a vital role in targeted interaction as this helps the work get done more efficiently. Assigning specific roles to team members that align with their strengths and capabilities results in a high quality and faster end result.
I have demonstrated this learning outcome through asking my audience for feedback in order to understand expectations and needs. This was done through user research and asking my fellow international students for what they expect to see on a “Housing Tips” website for our group project. Hearing everyone’s input and experiences with looking for housing helped us create a more tailored website that will be very helpful. From this user research, I created User Personas to help our team have an audience-centric approach to our project.

Reflection:

To reflect on this learning outcome, I am believe I could have been more active in communicating with my stakeholders to gain a better understanding of expectations. For example, I should have asked my teachers for more feedback on my portfolio to avoid confutation as to what was expected of me. However, I am pleased with my progress in this learning outcome as I have noticed a increase in my communication and engaging skills since the beginning of the semester. Going forward, I will focus on these aspects in order to be more successful in my group and individual work.
